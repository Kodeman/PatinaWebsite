The Refinement

Subtle Craft, Not Obvious Decoration

The goal isn't to add craft "imagery" — it's to make the entire experience feel handmade. These elements work subconsciously, creating a sense that real people made this, without ever being distracting.

Paper Grain Texture

A barely-visible noise overlay on hero sections suggests natural materials — like viewing content on fine cotton paper rather than a sterile screen.

2-3% opacity noise

Organic Background Shapes

Soft, asymmetric forms in the background replace rigid geometric patterns. Shapes feel hand-drawn — imperfect, warm, alive.

Provenance Lines

Brief maker attributions appear in hero sections — a quiet reminder that real hands shaped these pieces. Always present, never shouting.

Crafted in Vermont since 1904

Material Touch Points

Small material swatches and origin badges appear on imagery — connecting digital visuals to physical reality.
