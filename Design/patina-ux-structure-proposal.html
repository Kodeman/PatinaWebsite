UI/UX Structural Proposal

Structure Should Feel Like Voice

The warmest copy in the world can't save a page that feels like a SaaS product tour. Here's how to restructure patina.cloud so the layout itself embodies "tactile over technical, stories over specs, journey over task."

The Core Tension

The Current Structure Contradicts the Voice

What the Brand Says

  • "Tactile Over Technical" ‚Äî Every interaction should feel like touching fabric swatches
  • "Stories Over Specs" ‚Äî Lead with provenance and craftsmanship, not features
  • "Journey Over Task" ‚Äî Frame scanning as "walking your room together," not "capture data"
  • "Technology should be invisible" ‚Äî Let users focus on what matters

Structural Recommendations

Nine Changes That Matter Most

Each recommendation addresses a specific tension between the current structure and Patina's voice principles. Collectively, they transform the page from a product tour into an invitation.

Recommendation 01

Remove "Powered by" from Hero

The hero's job is to create desire, not explain technology. "Powered by The Aesthete Engine" positions the tech as the product rather than the experience.

Instead: Let the hero be purely emotional. Save the "how" for later‚Äîor better, let users discover it themselves.

Invisible Technology Journey Over Task

Proposed Hero Copy

Where Time Adds Value

Discover furniture that grows more beautiful with every year. See it in your space. Know the hands that made it.

Recommendation 02

Add a "First Touch" Interactive Moment

Currently, the only action before the CTA is scrolling. For a brand about tactile experience, this is a missed opportunity.

Add: A simple interactive element early in the page‚Äîa mini style quiz, a material selector, or an AR preview teaser. Let users touch before they're asked to download.

Recommendation 03

Move Makers Section Up‚ÄîWay Up

The maker stories are Patina's differentiator, yet they're currently the 5th section. By the time users reach them, they've already scrolled through feature explanations.

Move makers to position 2 or 3. Lead with the human story. Let "Nordic Atelier, Est. 1968" create trust before explaining algorithms.

Recommendation 04

Add a "Material Stories" Sensory Interlude

The page has no moment of pure sensory immersion. Every section is informational. The brand guidelines call for "linen, leather, cedar, grain"‚Äîbut there's no place where users just feel materials.

Add: A full-bleed section with macro photography of materials‚Äîweathered oak grain, aged leather, raw linen weave. Minimal text. Maximum texture.

Recommendation 05

Collapse or Remove "The Intelligence" Section

This is the biggest structural contradiction. The brand says "technology should be invisible," but an entire section explains the algorithm in detail.

Option A: Remove entirely. Let the tech be truly invisible.
Option B: Collapse into an expandable "How it works" link for curious users.
Option C: Move to a dedicated page, link subtly from footer.

Recommendation 06

Redesign "The Experience" as a Journey, Not Steps

The current 4-step numbered process reads like a product tour: "Step 1: Walk your room together. Step 2: See what fits." This is task-oriented, not journey-oriented.

Redesign as: A flowing visual narrative without numbers. Show the journey as a continuous experience, not discrete steps. Use scroll-triggered animations to reveal each moment organically.

Recommendation 07

Distribute Testimonials Throughout

All testimonials are currently in one carousel section. Carousels have notoriously low engagement, and grouping all social proof together feels like a "testimonials" checkbox.

Distribute: Place relevant quotes near the content they validate. Sarah Chen's AR quote should appear near AR content. Rachel K's "designer notes" quote belongs near maker content.

Recommendation 09

Elevate: Give professional services its own visual moment‚Äîperhaps a different background treatment, larger imagery, or a more prominent position. This is the "when you're ready for more" moment that deserves ceremony.

The Underlying Principle

Every structural decision should answer: "Does this feel like walking through a design studio with material samples in hand, or does it feel like a software product tour?" If it's the latter, restructure.
